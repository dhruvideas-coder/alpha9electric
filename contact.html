Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are my lights flickering?

Flickering lights are usually a sign of an electrical overload or a loose connection somewhere in the circuit. It may seem like a minor annoyance, but it can point to a more serious underlying issue. To stay safe, we recommend having a certified electrician inspect the problem as soon as possible.

Why is my light switch hot to the touch?

Every light switch is rated to handle a specific amount of electricity. If it feels hot, that's a warning sign — it could mean a faulty switch, damaged wiring, or an overloaded circuit. A hot switch is a serious safety hazard and should be inspected by a licensed electrician right away. Don't ignore it.

How do I know if my electrical outlet is burning?

There are a few clear warning signs: you may notice discoloration or scorch marks around the outlet, a burning smell nearby, or the outlet may feel unusually hot when you touch it. Any one of these signs means the outlet could cause electric shock or even start a fire.

If you suspect your outlet is burning, stop using it and call a licensed electrician immediately.

What is a GFCI outlet?

GFCI stands for Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter. These are special safety outlets designed to protect you from electric shock when using electrical devices near water — such as in bathrooms, kitchens, or outdoors. They work by automatically cutting off power the moment they detect a dangerous surge. If your home doesn't have GFCI outlets in the required areas, we strongly recommend getting them installed.
